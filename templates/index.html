Score Calculation

How The Final Score Is Computed

For participant \(p\), the normalized weighted score is:

\[ ext{Score}_p = \left( \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n}\left(\frac{\text{solve}_{p,i}}{\max\_\text{solve}_i}\right)\,w_i}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i} \right) \times 100 \]

Where \(\max\_\text{solve}_i\) is the highest solve count in contest \(i\), \(\text{solve}_{p,i}\) is participant \(p\)'s solve count in contest \(i\), and \(w_i\) is contest \(i\)'s weight. Since each ratio \(\text{solve}_{p,i}/\max\_\text{solve}_i\le 1\), the final score is always at most \(100\). If a participant is top solver in every contest, each ratio is \(1\), so the final score is exactly \(100\).
